National Cycling Centre Hamilton

A legacy of the 2003 UCI World Road Cycling Championships in Hamilton Ontario.

One of five nationally recognized cycling centres in Canada responsible for identifying, recruiting, testing, coaching and training high performance and development athletes in Ontario. Formally partnered with the Canadian Cycling Association, the Ontario Cycling Association, the City of Hamilton and McMaster University. Since its creation in 2005, the Centre has coached over 75 athletes to medals at National Championships and/or to become members of the Canadian National Cycling Team!

The NCCH has also partnered with key organizations to form the very first Para Cycling Centre in Canada!

Disciplines

Anything on a bike is good!

Road

Our centre in Ancaster is at the Western edge of Hamilton and surrounded by incredible quiet country rodes where we train. We race locally, provincially, nationally, internationally.

Track

We ride and race at both the Mattamy National Cycling Centre velodrome in Milton and the Forest City Velodrome in London, Ontario

Mountain Bike

Our centre borders the Hamilton Conservation Authority with over 70km of trails for all abilities. Our riders compete at Milton, Ontario Cups, Canada Cups, Provincial and National Championships

Cyclocross

Mud! Mud! Mud! Our riders compete in Ontario Cups, Provincial and National Championships, and World Cup races.
